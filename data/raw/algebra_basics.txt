Basic Algebra Overview

Algebra uses symbols and variables to represent numbers in equations.
A variable is often written as x, y, or n.

A linear equation has the form ax + b = c where a, b, and c are constants.
To solve for x, isolate the variable by performing inverse operations on both sides.

Example:
2x + 3 = 11
2x = 8
x = 4

Slope-intercept form is y = mx + b.
The value m is the slope and b is the y-intercept.
